The WTO Chairs Programme (WCP) Circular Economy Week

KEY THEME

Showcasing Practices and Ideas of Circular Economy to Forge Knowledge Co-Production on International Trade and Sustainability Linkages in Developing Countries

BACKGROUND

Through its Chairs Programme, the World Trade Organization (WTO) is fostering research, training and outreach activities of relevance to governmental policy makers and other stakeholders at universities in developing countries and least developing countries (LDCs). In 2021, a special call was made to the Chairs of the first and second phases inviting them to submit research proposals for projects focused on the following themes:

1. COVID -19 and Global Value Chains: Keeping Global Markets Open

2. Promoting Economic Recovery through Trade in a post COVID-19 World

3.Towards a Sustainable and Greener Trade: Trade Environment, Fisheries and Aquaculture in the context of the Blue Economy, Trade and Climate Change

4. E-Commerce and the Digital Economy

5. Inclusive Trade: Micro, Small, Medium-Sized Enterprises (MSMEs), Women and Trade

With support from the WCP donors, 12 Chairs from universities in Argentina, Brazil, Barbados, Chile, China, Indonesia, Jordan, Kenya, Mauritius, Mexico, Morocco and Turkey received funding. Indonesia (under the Center for World Trade Studies, Universitas Gadjah Mada) has been a Chair with the WCP since its first phase was launched in 2010. For this special call, the Chair implemented a project focused on expanding and promoting knowledge co-production in developing countries on the linkages of the WTO, international trade policy and the circular economy (CE) from regional and subnational perspectives.

EVENT DESCRIPTION

1. Circular Economy Week (CEW) Exhibition

Circular Economy Week is a trade expo for circular economy practitioners of the Indonesia Consortium for Circular Economy (ICCE), facilitated by the Center for World Studies UGM. This exhibition aims to show the general public the efforts and good practices of the circular economy in order to actualize sustainable development. Furthermore, MSME products and profiles will be showcased in promotional videos that will be shown throughout the exhibition. To create a festive atmosphere, there will also be musical performances from UGM internal performers at either the faculty and university levels. In addition to the exhibition, Circular Economy Week hosts informative booths and invites relevant guests to participate in two-way discussions about the circular economy concept.

2. Movie Screening Representing Southeast Asia, Latin America, and the Caribbean Socioeconomic Conditions

The event takes the form of a film screening produced in either Southeast Asia, Latin America, or the Caribbean region which will be followed by a film discussion regarding the substance. Continuing the sub-themes discussed for the 2nd FEALAC SEALAC Academic Dialogue Forum, this event will seek to convey messages about the political development landscape and/or digital transformation conditions in the region, but with a more popular and, hopefully, down-to-earth packaging. The three-hour film screening is expected to bring audiences to explore the region’s journey regarding political development and digital transformation which then, at the same time, confirm sustainable ways to create 3P-oriented digital transformation, namely People, Planet, and Prosperity.

3. Talk Show Regarding Climate Issues

The talk show is one of a series of Circular Economy Week (CEW) events that invite speakers specializing in environmental issues and collective movements to tackle climate change. The talk show will be accompanied by creative and interesting performances by the speakers aimed at internalizing ecological values and the climate change movement in accordance with the circular economy concept. This event is expected to be able to build awareness as well as encourage the audience to contribute through a circular economy scheme to minimize the impact of climate change. As a result, this talk show can serve as a forum for knowledge expansion and an inclusive discussion space for the public.

❊ Research and policy advocacy to adopt sustainability and circular economy concepts in trade and industrial practices of developing countries—seen from SEA-LAC region experiences, both at national and sub-national levels.

OBJECTIVES

×

❊ To disseminate research on international practices which follow stronger CE principles

❊ Conduct grassroot level studies and documentation of Indonesian entrepreneurial CE practices

❊ To encourage implementation of national and subnational policies reflecting CE principles and practices

SCOPES

×

❊ Policies and Governance of CE
Covering areas of CE-related regulatory framework and practices, at global, national and subnational levels; how they affect policies on the environment, sustainable development and climate change; how relevant stakeholders respond and initiate novel ideas, technical solutions and technological as well as policy innovation

❊ Educating CE
The scope in which CE principles is adopted as training program and awareness campaign at various educational institutions and academic programs; how they design and develop curriculum and extracurricular activities which are in line with the adoption of CE principles

❊ CE Socio-Cultural Dimensions
Contextualizing concepts and practices of CE based on specific, localized and home-grown socio-cultural values, traditions and habits; how such contexts contribute to empowerer community in their CE engagement, upgrade their capacity in upstream, midstream and downstream economic activities which are based on CE principles
